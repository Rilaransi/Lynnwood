FAQs

FAQs

Currently, our clinic does not offer walk-in services. We are reviewing our patient care model and may offer walk-ins in the future. Please check back for updates, or contact us directly to schedule an appointment.
Please bring a valid government-issued photo ID (e.g., driver’s license, passport) and your health care card, ensuring it is valid for your visit.
Patients without a provincial health care card will be billed privately. A government-issued photo ID is still required.
Ask your doctor if a follow-up visit is needed. Typically, a follow-up is required if symptoms persist or worsen. You can also schedule an appointment to discuss your results in person.
To receive a specialist referral, schedule an appointment with your family physician, who will provide the referral if necessary.
The following services are not covered by Alberta Health Services (this is not a full list):
- Sick notes
- Form completion
- Doctor’s notes/certificates
To release your medical information, you must provide written, witnessed authorization. This service is not covered by Alberta Health Services and will be billed privately.
In an emergency, call 911 or go to the nearest emergency room. For non-emergency health concerns, you can contact:
- HealthLink Alberta: 811
- Health and Wellness Alberta
- Health Canada
- Public Health Agency of Canada
